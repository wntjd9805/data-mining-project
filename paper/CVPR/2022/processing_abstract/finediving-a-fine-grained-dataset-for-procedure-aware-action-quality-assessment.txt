Most existing action quality assessment methods rely on the deep features of an entire video to predict the score, which is less reliable due to the non-transparent inference process and poor interpretability. We argue that under-standing both high-level semantics and internal temporal structures of actions in competitive sports videos is the key to making predictions accurate and interpretable. Towards this goal, we construct a new fine-grained dataset, calledFineDiving, developed on diverse diving events with de-tailed annotations on action procedures. We also propose a procedure-aware approach for action quality assessment, learned by a new Temporal Segmentation Attention module.Specifically, we propose to parse pairwise query and ex-emplar action instances into consecutive steps with diverse semantic and temporal correspondences.
