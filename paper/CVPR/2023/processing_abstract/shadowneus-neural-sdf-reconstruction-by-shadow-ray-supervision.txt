By supervising camera rays between a scene and multi-view image planes, NeRF reconstructs a neural scene rep-resentation for the task of novel view synthesis. On the other hand, shadow rays between the light source and the scene have yet to be considered. Therefore, we propose a novel shadow ray supervision scheme that optimizes both the samples along the ray and the ray location. By su-pervising shadow rays, we successfully reconstruct a neu-ral SDF of the scene from single-view images under mul-tiple lighting conditions. Given single-view binary shad-ows, we train a neural network to reconstruct a complete scene not limited by the cameraâ€™s line of sight. By further modeling the correlation between the image colors and the shadow rays, our technique can also be effectively extended to RGB inputs. We compare our method with previous works on challenging tasks of shape reconstruction from single-view binary shadow or RGB images and observe signif-icant improvements.
