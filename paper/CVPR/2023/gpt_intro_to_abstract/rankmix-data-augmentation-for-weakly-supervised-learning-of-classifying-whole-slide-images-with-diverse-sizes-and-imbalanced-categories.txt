The introduction of a computer science paper typically serves as a concise overview of the research objective and key components of the study. It provides the context and motivation for the research topic, outlines the problem statement, and summarizes the main contributions of the paper. The introduction also highlights the significance and relevance of the research in the broader field of computer science. Moreover, it may provide a brief outline of the paper's structure, indicating the flow and organization of the subsequent sections. By reading the introduction, readers can gain a preliminary understanding of the research scope and objective, enabling them to decide whether the paper aligns with their interests and to anticipate the specific insights that are covered in the subsequent sections.
